History of Video Games

Video games are digital games that can be played on a computer device (e.g., tablets, smartphones, laptops, consoles, etc.). Originally designed simply for entertainment, video games have grown into their own industry and field of learning, generating billions of dollars each year.

The Beginning (1950s)

  • In 1950 the basic guidelines for programming a chess-playing computer was laid out by a man named Claude Shannon. Later that year both him and Alan Turing created chess programs.
  • In 1954 Programmers at new Mexico's Los Alamos laboratories developed the first blackjack program on an IBM-701 computer.
  • In 1957 Alex Bernstein writes the first compelte computer chess program on an IBM-704 computer. The program was so advanced that it was able to evaluate four half-moves ahead.

Video Games at Home (1960-1983)

  • In 1967 Ralph baer developes the "Brown Box", which was a video game prototype that let users play tennis and other games.
  • In 1972 Nolan Bushnell and Al Alcorn of Atari developed an arcade table tennis game by the name of Pong.
  • In 1975 the home version of Pong was introduced by Atari.

The Rise of Games in the East (1985-1995)

  • In 1989 Nintendo's Game Boy makes handheld gaming popular. Its good game play, ease of use, and long battery life made users love it.
  • In 1991 Sonic The Hedgehog was introduced by Sega for the Sega Genesis.
  • In 1994 Blizzard realesed Warcraft: Orcs and Humans.

Tech Company Takeover (1995-2010)

  • In 1997 Electronic Arts bought out Maxis for $125,000,000.
  • In 1998 Electronic Arts bought out Westwood Studios for $122,500,000
  • In 1999 Lycos bought out Gamesville for $207,000,000

Today in Gaming (2010-Now)

  • In 2010 the very popular indie game "Minecraft" from the Swedish developer Markus Persson, sparked the indie game movement
  • In 2011 Skylanders: Spyro's Adventure becomes the first augmented-reality hit by letting players use small plastic figures to place them in the game and choose different characters.
  • In 2013 games like Gone Home, The Last of Us, an dpapers, Please start a new wave of mature video game stories that face players with tough emotional choices.
  • In 2014 "Free-to-play" starts to be a dominant business with games like CrossFire, Clash of Clans, and World of Tanks.
  • In 2017 the Nintendo Switch was introduced as the first hybrid mobile/home video game console into a game market that was dominated by smartphones and tablets.
